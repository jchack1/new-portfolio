I am a full stack web developer and designer. I am a creative visual and technical problem-solver with a passion for exceptional user experiences.

My Story

I come from a varied background and I have many different interests. My curiosity for science led me to study at the University of Calgary and earn a Bachelor of Science degree. After focusing on science for the first two years, I found that I missed doing creative work, as I grew up drawing, painting, and taking art classes. I decided to pursue a minor in art. This was the best decision I could have made, as it gave my program balance and revived a passion for creativity that I had lost for a while. I found a community within my printmaking courses. Sometimes I found it difficult to bring together the two fields I enjoyed, and I frequently explored these ideas in my printmaking and photography work.

Upon graduation I struggled to find work related to science due to the economic downturn, so I broadened my job search. I worked for a few years in administrative jobs in the banking industry and was promoted to a more senior role in my branch. I enjoyed the problem-solving aspect of my job and being the branch detective, but ultimately I found that my passion was not in banking.

Ideally I wanted to do work that would combine my creative and analytical sides. I wanted to challenge my mind and solve problems, similar to my previous work. After a lot of time spent thinking, researching, attending meet-ups, and experimenting with coding tutorials, I decided to pursue web development. This seemed like the perfect combination of all my interests, so I attended the Web Developer program at SAIT to further my skills. I had fun building projects with code, seeing the text I typed in an editor translate into a beautiful visual design or help accomplish a useful task.

While creating my projects I also found that I loved the design process. The visual side of design - like typography, composition, color palettes, working with negative space - came natually with my background in art. With more practice and time spent working in the industry as a full stack web developer I became more passionate about user experience design and creating useful, purposeful products that ease frustration and make people's day-to-day lives easier.

All of my experiences and interests have brought me to what I am doing today. There is still a lot to learn, but I am excited to continue learning and improving my skills.
